What Is Gentrification?

The term, “Gentrification”, was coined by British sociologist Ruth Glass in 1964: “One by one, many of the working class quarters of London have been invaded by middle classes—upper and lower. Shabby, modest mews and cottages—two rooms up and two down—have been taken over, when their leases have expired, and have become more elegant, expensive residences” (Glass, 1964) According to Glass’s description, there are two main characteristics of gentrification: (1) sharp increase of house prices and rent in inner cities and (2) displacement, which involves an upper middle class population displacing a working class population.
